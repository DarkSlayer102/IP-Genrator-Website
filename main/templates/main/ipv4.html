IPV4

IPv4 is the fourth version of the Internet Protocol, used to identify devices on a network. IPv4 addresses are composed of four sets of numbers ranging from 0 to 255, separated by periods. There are approximately 4.3 billion unique IPv4 addresses available. Each device connected to the internet requires a unique IPv4 address to communicate and exchange data. IPv4 addresses are gradually being supplemented by IPv6 addresses to meet the growing demand for internet-connected devices.
